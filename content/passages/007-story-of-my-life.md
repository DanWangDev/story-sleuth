  sight and hearing. Accessible first-person prose with universal
  themes of family and early memory. Strong for inference questions
  about what the author implies (her contrast of "blissful ignorance"
  before the illness is heavy with meaning). Great GL-style retrieval
  questions: where she was born, her father's and mother's origins,
  her first words. Bridges fiction and non-fiction sensibilities —
  autobiography reads like a story. Good choice for students who find
  straight informational non-fiction dry.
